What is SGPA?

SGPA stands for Semester Grade Point Average. It is a measure of a student's academic performance during a single semester. Universities and colleges use SGPA to evaluate how well a student has performed in all subjects during that semester.

How is SGPA Calculated?

SGPA is calculated by dividing the total quality points earned by the total credit hours attempted during the semester.

Formula:

SGPA = Σ(Credit Hours × Grade Points) ÷ Total Credit Hours

Why is SGPA Important?

SGPA helps students monitor their academic progress semester by semester. It is often used for scholarships, academic awards, internships, and admission to higher education programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SGPA the same as CGPA?
No. SGPA represents performance in a single semester, while CGPA represents overall performance across multiple semesters.

Can SGPA be converted into CGPA?
Yes. CGPA is usually calculated using the SGPAs of all semesters.
