What is CGPA?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the overall academic performance of a student across all completed semesters. It provides a comprehensive measure of academic achievement throughout a degree program.

How is CGPA Calculated?

CGPA is calculated by multiplying each semester's SGPA by its credit hours, adding all quality points together, and dividing by total credit hours.

CGPA = Σ(SGPA × Credit Hours) ÷ Total Credit Hours

Why is CGPA Important?

Universities, scholarship committees, employers, and graduate schools often evaluate students based on their CGPA. A higher CGPA can improve opportunities for internships, scholarships, and admissions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between SGPA and CGPA?

SGPA measures performance in a single semester, while CGPA measures overall academic performance across all semesters.

Is a 4.0 CGPA good?

Yes, a 4.0 CGPA generally represents excellent academic performance.
